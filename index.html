What are indoor plants? Indoor plants are plants that grow indoors. There are a variety of tropical plants, like palms, that thrive in indoor environments.

How do you plant indoor plants? Most of the time, indoor plants are already in containers, so there is no need to plant them. There are typically only two reasons you need to plant an indoor plant.
  1. If your plant is getting too big, then you will need to replant it into a larger container.
  2. If you wanted to grow bulbs indoors, then you will need to plant the bulbs yourself.

What are examples of some common indoor plants?
More examples of common indoor plants include the following:
  • Aglaonema
  • Dracaena
  • Ferns
  • Philodendrons
  • Palms
How do you care for indoor plants?
Here are some tips that will help you care for indoor plants:
  • Keep the potting soil moist- It’s important to make sure soil is not too wet nor too dry
  • Make sure the plant pot has drainage holes in the bottom of the pot
  • Place your plant near a light source, whether it’s natural or artificial
  • Determine what species of plant you have so you can more accurately care for it

Outdoor Plants
Outdoor plants are a bit different from indoor plants. They need to be able to withstand the elements, so they need to be tougher. Outdoor plants will not thrive if they are kept indoors. Plus, outdoor plants require more maintenance than indoor plants.
Examples of Outdoor Plants:
  • Hydrangeas
  • Roses
  • Lilacs
  • Herbs
  • Chrysanthemums
Best Methods of Care
Caring for outdoor plants is different from indoor plants. Outdoor plants need to be watered more often because they are exposed to the elements.
Below are some useful tips on how to care for outdoor plants effectively:
  • Water your plants in the morning so they have all day to absorb the water.
  • Check the soil before watering to see if it is dry. If it is, then water your plant.
  • Fertilize your plants every two weeks with a balanced fertilizer.
  • Deadhead spent flowers to encourage new growth.
  • Pruning is also important for outdoor plants. Trim back dead or dying branches to encourage new growth.

What is a succulent?
Succulents are plants that have telltale fleshy leaves containing sap. The reason that they have those leaves is so they hold as much moisture as possible. Where they are found in desert areas, but can be found in multiple places around the globe.

Succulents cover a wide variety of types of plants. Aloe plants, cacti and even orchids can be considered succulents. ZZ plants and other common indoor plants are usually in the succulent family. They are popular because they are relatively low maintenance, require little watering or pruning and some species need very little light to thrive.

Basic caring for succulents
What are some succulent garden planting tips?
As with any garden, you have to choose plants that appeal to you. Whether that garden is indoors or outdoors, choosing what types of plants you want is a personal decision. If you have decided to start a succulent garden, that advice applies, as well. Choose the ones that you like and that appeal to aesthetically.

The other tip would be to watch how often you water the plants. Remember that succulents by their very nature do not need much water. If you have them indoors and have your succulents in saucers, toss away the excess water that gathers there after you water them. If you chose a variety of air plant, just mist the plants.

Beyond that, make sure that you read the information that comes with the plants and ask questions of the plant or garden experts to make sure you know how to care for any and all plants you want to put into your garden.

What are flowering shrubs? A flowering shrub is a type of woody plant that is characterized by its ability to produce flowers. These plants are typically smaller than trees but larger than herbaceous plants, and they often have multiple stems or branches arising from the base. Flowering shrubs are popular in landscaping and gardening for their aesthetic appeal, as they can add color, texture, and fragrance to outdoor spaces. They are commonly used as hedges, borders, focal points, or even as standalone ornamental plants.
Some examples of popular flowering shrubs include:
  • Roses
  • Lilacs
  • Spirea
  • Camellias
  • Weigela
Maintenance of flowering shrubs:
  • Watering: Keep soil consistently moist, not soggy.
  • Mulching: Apply mulch to retain moisture and deter weeds.
  • Fertilizing: Feed in spring with balanced fertilizer.
  • Pruning: Trim for shape and remove dead growth.
  • Deadheading: Trim spent flowers for new growth.
  • Protection: Guard from pests, diseases, and harsh weather.
  • >Monitoring: Regularly inspect for issues and act promptly.
  • Winter Care: Mulch base, consider winter protection.
    • Adapt care based on shrub type and local conditions.
